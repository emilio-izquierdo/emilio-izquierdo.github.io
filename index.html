Emilio Izquierdo

Recent graduate, Case Western Reserve University

My research interests center on individual differences in psychological outcomes, exploring why people vary in symptom severity and affective processing, as well as what physiological, behavioral, and computational approaches can reveal about the mechanisms underlying that variance.

Diagnostic categories are contested, presentations vary enormously, and treatments that work well on average still leave a meaningful subset without clear next steps. That isn't a failure of the field so much as a sign that the average is only part of the answer. All of this has pushed me toward smaller, more mechanistic questions and toward building the tools to ask them.

→ Research

This same philosophy shapes how I approach my own hobbies and habits. We are at a unique point where the tools required to track, model, and optimize learning on an individual level are entirely accessible. Rather than adopting generalized methods, I am deeply interested in the mechanics of how we adapt and process information as individuals. I aim to make personalized learning a tangible reality rather than just a theoretical concept.
