Physiological Mechanisms of Stress and Cognitive Adaptation

People vary in how they adapt to stress, both physiologically and cognitively. This project examines that variance by measuring cardiovascular and respiratory stress responses during an acute stressor and tracking how individual differences in those responses predict changes in attentional control on a conflict task.

Differential Prediction of PTSD Symptom Clusters by Emotion Regulation Difficulties

PTSD is typically studied and treated as a uniform construct, but its symptom clusters are mechanistically distinct. This project took a symptom-first approach, asking whether specific emotion regulation difficulties map onto specific clusters rather than onto PTSD severity broadly.
