Physiological Mechanisms of Stress and Cognitive Adaptation

This project measures cardiovascular stress responses during an acute stressor and asks whether individual differences in those responses predict how conflict processing breaks down, both at the behavioral level and in terms of the underlying cognitive mechanisms modeled computationally using drift diffusion models. No validated open-source tooling existed for impedance cardiography, so the pipeline was built from scratch.

Differential Prediction of PTSD Symptom Clusters by Emotion Regulation Difficulties

PTSD is typically studied and treated as a uniform construct, but its symptom clusters are mechanistically distinct. This project took a symptom-first approach, asking whether specific emotion regulation difficulties map onto specific clusters rather than onto PTSD severity broadly.
